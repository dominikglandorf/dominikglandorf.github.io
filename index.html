Dominik Glandorf

Hey, I am graduate student of Quantitative Data Science Methods at the University of Tübingen, Germany, with formal training in computer science and psychology. Currently, I am based in New Haven, CT, where I do research and coursework at Yale University, more specifically at the Cognitive and Neural Computation Lab by Ilker Yildirim. My passion is Educational Data Science and, more broadly, Artificial Intelligence in Education. I believe that generative AI can have a positive impact on learning experiences and promote equity if used thoughtfully.

Projects

Grammar teaching with LLM

I am working on how to use the power of LLMs in a pedagogically reasonable way. Therefore, I investigated whether transformer-based embeddings represent grammar and text generation can be controlled in terms of grammar. You can find a course project on this topic on my GitHub.

InterestLearn

I created InterestLearn, a language learning tool designed to engage students with personalized content and adaptive interaction. Check out the demo!

Prerequisite mining

For my Master's research seminar, I investigated prerequisite structures of knowledge concepts and how to extract them from textbooks, Wikipedia and LLMs. Preliminary results have shown that small language models are not that good at this task.

Work

With Christian Fischer I work on an early warning system for student dropout in higher education. Our work will be presented at LAK24 in March! I also worked on the data-privacy-compliant integration of third-party education resources into the dBildungscloud using a pseudonymization approach.
